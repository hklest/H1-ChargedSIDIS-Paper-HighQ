\begin{abstract}
Inclusive charged-hadron multiplicities and the charge asymmetry are measured in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) at high $Q^2 > 150$~GeV$^2$ using the H1 detector at HERA. The analysis utilizes the HERA-II dataset with an integrated luminosity of $\Lumi = 351$~pb$^{-1}$ from the four-period S67 target, including the ep0304 period. The multiplicities $M^{h\pm}$ and the charge asymmetry $A_h = (N_{h^+} - N_{h^-}) / (N_{h^+} + N_{h^-})$ are extracted using Iterative Bayesian Unfolding (IBU), with TUnfold employed as a diagnostic cross-check. The final results are presented in a 490-bin configuration (245 bins per charge) corresponding to the z005 release. Radiative corrections are applied based on DJANGOH14. The measurements are compared with predictions from SHERPA3 and PYTHIA8 MC generators.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) provides a powerful probe of the fragmentation process and the internal structure of the nucleon. By detecting a leading hadron in the final state, it is possible to correlate the kinematics of the produced hadron with the kinematics of the scattered lepton, thereby accessing the fragmentation functions.

In this work, we present the measurement of inclusive charged-hadron production in the high-$\Qsq$ regime ($\Qsq > 150$~GeV$^2$). This kinematic region is particularly interesting for testing perturbative QCD (pQCD) predictions and understanding the evolution of fragmentation functions at large scales.

\section{The H1 Detector and Dataset}
\subsection{The H1 Detector}
The H1 detector was a general-purpose spectrometer at the HERA electron-proton collider. The central tracking detector and the calorimeter system allow for the precise reconstruction of the scattered lepton and the produced hadrons. 

\subsection{Dataset}
The analysis is based on the HERA-II dataset. We utilize the four-period S67 target data, reaching a total integrated luminosity of $\Lumi = 351$~pb$^{-1}$. This dataset includes the ep0304 run period, ensuring a comprehensive sample of high-$\Qsq$ events.

\section{Analysis Methodology}
\subsection{Kinematic Selection}
Events are selected based on the requirement $\Qsq > 150$~GeV$^2$. Standard DIS selection criteria are applied to ensure the quality of the reconstructed kinematics and to suppress background from non-DIS processes.

\subsection{Observables}
We measure the multiplicities of positively and negatively charged hadrons, denoted as $M^{h+}$ and $M^{h-}$. From these, the charge asymmetry $A_h$ is derived:
\begin{equation}
    A_h = \frac{N_{h^+} - N_{h^-}}{N_{h^+} + N_{h^-}}
\end{equation}
where $N_{h^\pm}$ represents the number of produced hadrons of charge $\pm$.

\subsection{Unfolding and Corrections}
To correct for detector effects, including resolution and acceptance, we employ Iterative Bayesian Unfolding (IBU). The unfolding is performed in a 490-bin configuration (z005 release), with 245 bins allocated per charge sign. As a robustness check, the TUnfold package is used as a diagnostic cross-check to ensure the stability of the unfolded distributions.

Radiative corrections are applied to the data to account for QED effects. The correction factors $\Crad$ are determined using the DJANGOH14 generator.

\section{Systematic Uncertainties}
The systematic uncertainties are evaluated by varying the selection criteria and the unfolding parameters. A significant contribution arises from the tracking efficiency; specifically, an integrated $-0.5\%$ tracking efficiency systematic is incorporated into the total uncertainty budget. Other sources include the uncertainty in the luminosity measurement and the model dependence of the unfolding process.

\section{Summary}
We have measured the inclusive charged-hadron multiplicities and the charge asymmetry in SIDIS at $\Qsq > 150$~GeV$^2$ using the H1 detector. The analysis utilized 351~pb$^{-1}$ of HERA-II data. The results were corrected for detector effects using IBU and for radiative effects using DJANGOH14. The measurements provide a stringent test for MC generators such as SHERPA3 and PYTHIA8 and contribute to the understanding of hadronization at high energy scales.
